# IC Memo Conventions

Acquisition-specific conventions that supplement the core `memo-writing` skill. Defines the IC memo structure, what each section must contain, and how risks should be framed.

## IC Memo Structure

See `memo-writing` (core) for the general memo template. This skill adds acquisition-specific requirements:

### Executive Summary Requirements
- **First sentence**: "[Property] is a [size] [type] in [market] offered at [$price] ([cap rate])."
- **Second paragraph**: The investment thesis in 2-3 sentences. Why this deal, why now.
- **Third paragraph**: Key return metrics (IRR, equity multiple, CoC) under base case.
- **Final line**: Clear recommendation with price target.

### Financial Exhibits
- Sources & Uses table
- 10-year pro forma summary (NOI, NCF, CFAD)
- Returns summary (unlevered and levered)
- Sensitivity matrix (exit cap vs. rent growth)
- Comp table (sales, with implied cap rates)

### Risk Section Format
Each risk must follow this format:
1. **Risk**: Specific, measurable statement (not "market risk" but "submarket has 2.1M SF under construction, representing 8% of inventory")
2. **Impact**: What happens if the risk materializes (quantified if possible)
3. **Mitigant**: How the risk is managed, monitored, or hedged
4. **Residual**: After mitigation, what exposure remains

### Minimum Risk Categories
- Market / supply risk
- Tenant concentration or credit risk
- Capital requirements / deferred maintenance
- Financing risk (rate, availability, refi)
- Environmental / regulatory
- Exit / liquidity risk
